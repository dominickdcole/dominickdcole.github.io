About this portfolio

I'm a mechanical engineering student at Kettering University headed toward aerospace structural design, and I've learned that engineering is only half technical. The other half is being understood — by a machinist, a hiring manager, a review committee, a reader who has never opened a textbook on the subject. Each of those people needs a different version of the same idea.

This portfolio is built to show one thing: that I can move between genres deliberately, not by accident. The three pieces collected here were written for very different purposes and very different audiences, and each one asked me to change my voice, my structure, and my evidence to match. Read together, they're meant to demonstrate range — the ability to choose a form that serves the reader rather than forcing every idea through the same template.

Each linked page introduces a piece, explains what genre it represents, and points to what that example is designed to show. A reflective personal narrative trades on honesty and arc. A discourse-community profile turns observation and interviews into a portrait of a working culture. A literature review subordinates voice entirely to synthesis and citation. The shift between them is the point.

Intended audience: prospective employers and engineering teams evaluating written communication alongside technical skill.
