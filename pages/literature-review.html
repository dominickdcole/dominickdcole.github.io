What this piece is

This is a literature review examining why morphing-wing technology — wings that continuously change shape instead of using hinged flaps — has never reached a commercial aircraft despite decades of favorable aerodynamic research. It organizes six peer-reviewed sources into three engineering barriers: the skin material problem, the embedded actuation problem, and the largely unstudied problem of storing fuel inside a flexing wing.

"The aerodynamic case is solid. The problem is everything that comes after the aerodynamics."

The genre, and why it's here

A literature review is the most formal and most impersonal genre in this portfolio — the opposite end of the spectrum from the personal statement. Voice recedes almost entirely. The author's job is not to argue a personal position but to map a body of research: grouping sources by theme, putting them in conversation with each other, and showing where they agree, diverge, and leave gaps.

The discipline here is structural and citational. Every claim is traceable to a source, the organization is thematic rather than source-by-source, and the writing has to stay precise without losing a reader who isn't a specialist. Synthesizing six independent studies into a single coherent narrative — rather than summarizing each one in turn — was the central challenge.

What it shows about my range

This piece demonstrates the formal, evidence-driven end of my writing: managing citations, synthesizing multiple sources responsibly, and sustaining a technical argument across several pages while keeping it readable.
