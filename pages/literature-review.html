What this piece is

This is a literature review that asks why morphing-wing technology has never reached a commercial aircraft. Morphing wings change shape continuously instead of using hinged flaps, and the aerodynamic research has long been favorable. The review organizes six peer-reviewed sources into three engineering barriers: the skin material problem, the embedded actuation problem, and the largely unstudied problem of storing fuel inside a wing that keeps flexing.

"The aerodynamic case is solid. The problem is everything that comes after the aerodynamics."

The genre, and why it's here

A literature review is the most formal and most impersonal genre in this portfolio. It sits at the opposite end of the spectrum from the personal statement. Voice almost disappears. The author's job is not to argue a personal position but to map a body of research. That means grouping sources by theme, putting them in conversation with each other, and showing where they agree, where they differ, and where they leave gaps.

The discipline here is structure and citation. Every claim traces back to a source. The organization is built around themes rather than one source at a time. The writing has to stay precise without losing a reader who is not a specialist. The hardest part was pulling six separate studies into one clear narrative instead of summarizing each one in turn.

What it shows about my range

This piece shows the formal, evidence-driven side of my writing. It manages citations, synthesizes several sources responsibly, and holds a technical argument together across several pages while keeping it readable.
