What this piece is

This is a personal statement written for aerospace employers. It is a first-person story that follows one thread: a childhood plane ride, a stretch of doubt during my automotive co-op, and a fluid-mechanics lab that brought my original goal back into focus.

"I had my face pressed against the window, watching the wings flex up and down without breaking."

The genre, and why it's here

The personal narrative is the most personal genre in this portfolio, and the one that depends most on voice. A literature review hides the author behind the sources. A personal statement does the opposite. Here the writer's perspective is the evidence. The craft is in the structure. You build toward a turning point so the reader feels the realization instead of just being told about it.

I chose to anchor the piece on two concrete moments: a window seat at ten years old, and an airfoil in a wind tunnel years later. The link between them carries the argument that mechanical engineering was never a detour from aerospace, but the foundation for it. The goal was a story that earns its conclusion rather than simply stating it.

What it shows about my range

Set next to the other two pieces, this one shows that I can write in a personal voice with real honesty when the audience and purpose call for it. It also shows that I can shape lived experience into a clear arc instead of a plain list of events.
