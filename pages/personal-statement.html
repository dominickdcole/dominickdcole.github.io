What this piece is

This is a personal statement written for prospective aerospace employers — a first-person narrative that traces a single thread from a childhood plane ride, through a stretch of doubt during my automotive co-op, to a fluid-mechanics lab that pulled my original ambition back into focus.

"I had my face pressed against the window, watching the wings flex up and down without breaking."

The genre, and why it's here

Reflective narrative is the most personal genre in this portfolio, and the one most dependent on voice. Where a literature review hides the author behind the sources, a personal statement does the opposite: the writer's perspective is the evidence. The craft is in structure — building toward a turning point so the reader feels the realization rather than just being told about it.

I chose to anchor the piece on two concrete sensory moments — a window seat at ten years old and an airfoil in a wind tunnel years later — and let the connection between them carry the argument that mechanical engineering was never a detour from aerospace, but the foundation for it. The goal was a narrative that earns its conclusion instead of asserting it.

What it shows about my range

Read against the other two pieces, this one demonstrates that I can write with a personal register and emotional honesty when the audience and purpose call for it — and that I can shape lived experience into a deliberate arc rather than a chronological list.
